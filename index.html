Privacy Policy for LB Food Scanner

Last Updated: April 9, 2026

Thank you for using LB Food Scanner ("the App"). This Privacy Policy explains how the App handles information, what third-party services it may use, and what choices you have. This page also includes basic terms of use for the App.

1. The Core Principle

The App is built to be privacy-friendly.

  • No account is required to use the App.
  • No third-party ads are shown in the App.
  • The majority of your data, including scan history, saved product notes, allergen preferences, scan mode, and most settings, is stored only on your device.
  • Some features depend on third-party services such as product databases, translation services, chemical reference sources, or your device’s built-in speech services. Those services may receive limited data needed to provide the feature you chose to use.

2. Information We Process and How We Use It

To provide its features, the App needs to access or process certain information.

a. Information processed on your device

This information is primarily handled locally on your device.

  • Camera Access:
    • Why we need it: The App’s main features are barcode scanning and ingredient / INCI text scanning.
    • How it is used: The camera is only used when you actively scan a product or take a photo for ingredient recognition.
    • On-device processing: Depending on your platform and App version, barcode scanning, text recognition, language identification, and related features may use Google ML Kit on Android and/or Apple system frameworks such as Vision or related on-device tools on iOS.
    • Temporary files: Temporary image files or cache files may be created during scan processing or image handling on your device.
  • Scan History:
    • What it is: The App can save recently scanned products so you can view them again later.
    • How it is stored: Stored only on your device’s app storage.
    • Your control: You can disable history saving or clear saved history in the App settings.
  • Per-Product Notes:
    • What it is: You can save your own note for a scanned food or cosmetic product, for example to remember missing allergen or sensitive-ingredient details from the real packaging.
    • How it is stored: Notes are saved locally on your device only, usually per barcode or product fingerprint.
    • Who can see it: Only the user of that device/app install. Notes are not shared with other users.
  • Translation Cache:
    • What it is: The App may store cached translations locally to avoid repeating translation requests and to improve speed.
    • How it is stored: Stored only on your device.
  • Preferences and Settings:
    • Examples include selected allergens, nutritional alert toggles, preferred translation engine, voice mode, speech rate and pitch, dark mode, and selected scan mode.
    • These are stored locally on your device.
  • Voice Assistant / Text-to-Speech:
    • If you enable Voice Assistant Mode, the App sends text to your device’s configured speech engine so results can be read aloud.
    • Depending on your platform and version, this may use Android Text-to-Speech, Apple speech / voice services, or another installed device speech engine.
    • Your device’s speech engine may operate under its own privacy policy and terms.

b. Information shared with third-party services

Some features require limited data to be sent to third-party services. We do not require an account from you to use these features.

  • Food product barcode lookups:
    • When you scan a food barcode, the barcode may be sent to Open Food Facts and Pro Open Food Facts to retrieve product information.
    • If you manually enable the optional legacy source in Settings, the barcode may also be sent to FoodRepo.org.
  • Cosmetic product barcode lookups:
    • When you scan a cosmetic barcode, the barcode may be sent to Open Beauty Facts to retrieve product information.
  • Translation services:
    • Depending on your platform, app version, and chosen settings, translation may use Google ML Kit, Apple Translate, LibreTranslate, or Lingva Translate.
    • If you choose an online translator, the text you choose to translate may be sent to that selected third-party service.
    • Some translation models may be downloaded to your device when needed.
  • Cosmetic ingredient research features:
    • If you tap a cosmetic ingredient card to load extra reference data, the ingredient name may be sent to PubChem to retrieve chemical reference information.
    • If you choose to open CosIng, ECHA, or EWG Skin Deep, the App may open those services in your browser or direct you to their public pages. Your use of those sites is governed by their own terms and privacy policies.
  • Missing product reporting:
    • If you choose to report a missing food product, the scanned barcode may be sent to Open Food Facts / Pro Open Food Facts to help improve those databases.
  • Email contact / crash reporting by email:
    • If you choose to send us an email, your email app/provider will process your email address and message contents in the normal way.
    • We will receive whatever information you voluntarily include in that email and use it only to respond, investigate the issue, or provide support.

3. No Account, No Ads

  • The App does not require you to create an account.
  • The App does not show third-party ads.
  • The App is designed to work without a cloud profile or centralized user account.

4. Summary of Third-Party Services

The App may use or link to the following third-party tools and services, depending on your platform, app version, and settings:

  • Google ML Kit: Barcode scanning, OCR/text recognition, language identification, and on-device translation on supported Android versions.
  • Apple system frameworks / Apple Translate: Supported iOS scanning, text, translation, or accessibility features where available.
  • Android Text-to-Speech / Apple speech services: Reads product results aloud when voice features are used.
  • Open Food Facts / Pro Open Food Facts: Food product barcode data.
  • Open Beauty Facts: Cosmetic product barcode data.
  • FoodRepo.org: Optional legacy food barcode source if enabled by the user.
  • LibreTranslate / Lingva Translate: Optional translation providers selected by the user.
  • PubChem: Cosmetic ingredient chemical reference lookups.
  • CosIng, ECHA, and EWG Skin Deep: External reference and research resources that may be opened from the App.
  • Coil: Image loading library used inside the App.

5. Important Accuracy, Allergy, and Safety Notice

  • The App relies in part on third-party and community-maintained product databases. Product information can be incomplete, outdated, or inaccurate.
  • Allergen, ingredient, and sensitive-ingredient information may be missing from online database entries even when a product is found successfully.
  • The App may also generate warnings, ingredient insights, translations, voice summaries, or note-based reminders, but these are informational only.
  • Always check the real product packaging and manufacturer information, especially for allergies, intolerances, medical conditions, or skin sensitivities.
  • The App does not provide medical, nutritional, dermatological, regulatory, or legal advice.
  • Cosmetic and chemical reference tools such as PubChem, CosIng, ECHA, and EWG are provided for research convenience only and may have their own limitations, notices, and rules.

6. Your Rights and Choices

  • Camera Permission: You can deny or revoke camera access at any time in system settings.
  • History Controls: You can disable history saving and clear saved scan history inside the App.
  • Notes and Preferences: You can edit or clear your local product notes and settings inside the App.
  • Translation Choice: You can choose which translation service the App uses when multiple options are offered.
  • Voice Controls: You can disable voice mode or adjust speech settings at any time.
  • Local Data Removal: You can remove local App data by clearing App storage or uninstalling the App.

7. Children’s Privacy

The App is not intended for children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly seek to collect personal information from children through account creation or similar systems, because the App does not require an account.

8. Data Security

Because most App data is stored locally, the security of that data depends largely on your device’s own protections, such as screen lock, device encryption, and system security updates. No method of local storage or internet transmission can be guaranteed to be perfectly secure.
